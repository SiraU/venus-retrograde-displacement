## Abstract

This study examines the correlation between Venus retrograde periods and major episodes of forced displacement (exile, deportation, pogrom, ethnic cleansing) across 2,750 years (734 BCE to 2023 CE). Using the Swiss Ephemeris (pyswisseph 2.10.3, Moshier analytical ephemeris), we computed 1,768 Venus retrograde periods from 800 BCE to 2027 CE, identified five distinct 8-year synodic series, and tested 82 documented displacement events.

The primary finding is sign-independent: 11 of 82 displacement events (13.4%) occurred during a Venus retrograde period, against an expected rate of 7.2% (Venus spends approximately 42 of every 584 days in retrograde motion). A binomial test yields p = 0.033, reaching conventional statistical significance (p < 0.05). The 11 events span eight different zodiacal signs, from Gemini through Aquarius, indicating that the correlation operates at the level of retrograde motion itself, not at the level of any specific sign or series.

When the 82 events are distributed across the five 8-year synodic series, two series emerge above expected frequency: Series B (Capricorn, 22/82) driven by the 1941-1942 Holocaust-era cluster, and Series E (Scorpio-Libra, 17/82) with a broader historical distribution that includes the strongest single correlation in the dataset (Kristallnacht, November 9-10, 1938, day 10 of a Series E retrograde). However, no individual series reaches statistical significance; the significant signal appears only when all retrogrades are tested together.

Period-stratified analysis reveals that the dominant series shifts across historical periods in a pattern partially consistent with precessional drift through the zodiac: whichever series occupies Scorpio at a given era tends to dominate displacement events in that era.

**Keywords:** Venus retrograde, synodic cycle, mundane astrology, forced displacement, exile, Swiss Ephemeris, binomial test, correlation study

## 1. Introduction

The synodic cycle of Venus has been a subject of astrological observation since the Babylonian period. The MUL.APIN tablets (c. 1000 BCE) record Venus's appearances and disappearances, and the Ammisaduqa tablets (c. 1650 BCE) constitute some of the earliest systematic astronomical records in human history. In classical Hellenistic and medieval Islamic astrology, Venus governs beauty, art, social harmony, diplomacy, and the values by which a society defines itself.

In mundane astrology, Venus can be understood as a significator of social consensus: the shared agreements, aesthetic norms, and relational structures that bind a community together. When Venus is in retrograde motion, these agreements may come under review, revision, or disruption. This interpretation is consistent with the classical treatment of retrograde motion as an anomalous condition that inverts or complicates the planet's ordinary significations (Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos* III.4; Bonatti, *Liber Astronomiae* VI).

Forced displacement, including exile, deportation, pogrom, and ethnic cleansing, represents perhaps the most extreme form of social consensus disruption: the literal expulsion of a group from the body politic. If Venus retrograde marks periods when societies re-examine who belongs and who does not, we might expect forced displacement events to occur during Venus retrograde periods more frequently than chance would predict.

This study tests that hypothesis across 82 displacement events spanning 2,750 years.

### 1.1 Methodological Precedent

The author has previously conducted an empirical study analyzing 3,475 earthquakes against astrological parameters, finding no significant correlations (Uysal, 2026). The present study applies a similar empirical ethos: the goal is to document what the data shows, including null or ambiguous results, rather than to confirm a predetermined thesis.

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Astronomical Computation

All Venus retrograde stations between 800 BCE and 2027 CE were computed using the Swiss Ephemeris library (pyswisseph version 2.10.3, Moshier analytical ephemeris), in seven chronological segments with boundary deduplication.

**Total retrograde periods identified: 1,768** (800 BCE - 2027 CE)

### 2.2 Venus Retrograde Duration

Venus spends approximately 42 days in retrograde motion per synodic cycle of approximately 584 days, yielding a retrograde time fraction of 7.19%. If displacement events were randomly distributed in time, we would expect 7.19% of them to fall within a retrograde period.

### 2.3 Series Identification

Venus retrograde periods repeat in five zodiacal groups forming a pentagrammic pattern. Each retrograde recurs approximately every 8 years (2,919.6 days), shifted approximately 2-3 degrees earlier:

| Series | Current Sign | Approximate 700 BCE Sign |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| A | Gemini | Leo-Virgo |
| B | Capricorn | Aries-Pisces |
| C | Leo | Libra-Scorpio |
| D | Aries | Gemini-Cancer |
| E | Scorpio | Capricorn-Sagittarius |

### 2.4 Event Database

Eighty-two forced displacement events were compiled from 734 BCE to 2023 CE:

| Period | Events |
|--------|--------|
| Ancient (734 BCE - 415 CE) | 11 |
| Medieval (1096 - 1421) | 11 |
| Early Modern (1492 - 1755) | 9 |
| 19th Century (1821 - 1913) | 9 |
| 20th-21st Century (1915 - 2023) | 42 |

### 2.5 Statistical Methods

**Primary test:** Binomial test comparing the observed number of events during Venus retrograde against the expected proportion (p = 42/584 = 0.0719). One-tailed test: does displacement occur during retrograde more often than chance?

**Secondary tests:** Chi-square distribution of nearest-retrograde series assignments; DIRECT+PROXIMATE frequency by series; Monte Carlo distance-based correlation; period-stratified analysis.

## 3. Results

### 3.1 Primary Finding: Venus Retrograde and Displacement

Of 82 displacement events, 11 (13.4%) occurred during a Venus retrograde period:

| Event | Year | Retrograde Sign | Day of Retrograde |
|-------|------|----------------|-------------------|
| Assyrian deportation of Northern Israel | 734 BCE | Gemini 18° | Day 35 |
| Antiochus IV: Jerusalem persecution | 167 BCE | Capricorn 9° | Day 14 |
| First Crusade: Rhineland massacres | 1096 | Taurus 30° | Day 25 |
| Crusader conquest of Jerusalem | 1099 | Leo 6° | Day 15 |
| Cromwell: Irish Catholic deportation | 1652 | Libra 2° | Day 20 |
| Russian pogroms begin | 1881 | Taurus 21° | Day 3 |
| Nuremberg Race Laws | 1935 | Virgo 23° | Day 28 |
| **Kristallnacht** | **1938** | **Sagittarius 5°** | **Day 10** |
| Wannsee Conference | 1942 | Aquarius 21° | Day 7 |
| US Japanese internment | 1942 | Aquarius 21° | Day 37 |
| Baku pogrom | 1990 | Aquarius 6° | Day 15 |

**Binomial test: p = 0.033 (one-tailed). Statistically significant at p < 0.05.**

Expected: 5.9 events (7.19% of 82). Observed: 11. Ratio: 1.87x.

The 11 events span eight different zodiacal signs (Gemini, Capricorn, Taurus, Leo, Libra, Virgo, Sagittarius, Aquarius). The correlation is not specific to any single sign: it operates at the level of Venus retrograde motion itself.

### 3.2 PROXIMATE Matches

An additional 9 events occurred within 30 days of a retrograde boundary (before station retrograde or after station direct), bringing the total to 20/82 (24.4%) within the retrograde-plus-shadow window, against an expected 17.5% (binomial p = 0.070, marginal).

Notable PROXIMATE matches include Bar Kokhba expulsion (135 CE, 4 days before SR), Ottoman dissolution (1922, 3 days before SR), and Nakba (1948, 19 days before SR).

### 3.3 Secondary Finding: Series Distribution

When the 82 events are assigned to their nearest retrograde series, two series emerge above expected (16.4 per series):

| Series | Nearest Count | Deviation |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| A | 14 | -2.4 |
| **B** | **22** | **+5.6** |
| C | 14 | -2.4 |
| D | 15 | -1.4 |
| **E** | **17** | **+0.6** |

Chi-square = 2.756 (df = 4), p > 0.20. Not significant.

No individual series reaches significance. The significant signal (Section 3.1) appears only when all retrogrades are tested together, confirming that the correlation is a property of retrograde motion in general, not of any specific series.

### 3.4 Two Series, Two Patterns

Despite the lack of individual series significance, the above-expected counts of Series B and E exhibit distinct temporal signatures:

**Series B (Capricorn):** Concentrated in specific crisis periods. The 1941-1942 Holocaust cluster alone (Farhud, Iasi, Lviv, Jedwabne, Wannsee, Japanese internment) accounts for 6 of 22 nearest assignments. The Baku pogrom (1990) adds another DIRECT match. Signal is temporally narrow but event-dense.

**Series E (Scorpio-Libra):** Distributed broadly across centuries. Matches span from 1099 (Crusader Jerusalem) through 1890 (Wounded Knee), 1922 (Ottoman dissolution), 1938 (Kristallnacht), 1946 (Kielce), and 2018 (US border crisis). Signal is less concentrated but more historically consistent.

In the modern period (1915-2023), these two series together account for 23/42 events (55%), against an expected 40% for any two series.

### 3.5 Period-Stratified Analysis

| Period | Dominant Series |
|--------|----------------|
| Ancient (734 BCE - 415 CE) | B (5/11) |
| Medieval (1096-1421) | C and D tied (3/11 each) |
| Early Modern (1492-1755) | A (4/9) |
| 19th Century (1821-1913) | D (3/9) |
| Modern (1915-2023) | B (13/42) and E (10/42) |

The early modern dominance of Series A coincides with its transit through Scorpio. The modern dominance of Series E coincides with its current transit through Scorpio. This pattern is consistent with a zodiacal hypothesis: Venus retrograde in Scorpio (its detriment) may carry additional displacement-related weight, though the primary signal (Section 3.1) is sign-independent.

### 3.6 The Kristallnacht Correlation

The strongest individual correlation:

- Venus station retrograde: October 30, 1938, Sagittarius 4.9°
- Kristallnacht: November 9-10, 1938
- Venus station direct: December 10, 1938, Scorpio 19.6°
- Position: Day 10 of a 41-day retrograde (24%)

### 3.7 Robustness Analysis: Exact-Date Subset

A potential criticism of the primary finding is that ancient and medieval events carry date uncertainty of weeks or months, which could artificially inflate the retrograde coincidence rate. To address this, we restricted the analysis to the 43 events with day-level documented dates (primarily post-1572, where decrees, treaties, and news reports provide exact dates).

Of these 43 events, 6 occurred during a Venus retrograde:

| Event | Year | Sign |
|-------|------|------|
| Fall of Jerusalem to Crusaders | 1099 | Leo 6° |
| Nuremberg Race Laws | 1935 | Virgo 23° |
| Kristallnacht | 1938 | Sagittarius 5° |
| Wannsee Conference | 1942 | Aquarius 21° |
| Executive Order 9066 (Japanese internment) | 1942 | Aquarius 21° |
| Baku pogrom | 1990 | Aquarius 6° |

The ratio is 6/43 = 14.0%, compared to 11/82 = 13.4% in the full dataset. The retrograde-to-expected ratio is 1.94x, virtually identical to the full dataset's 1.87x. This confirms that the date-uncertain ancient events are not inflating the correlation.

However, the binomial p-value rises from 0.033 to 0.086 (marginal) due to reduced statistical power at n = 43. The ratio is stable; only the power to detect it diminishes with smaller sample size.

**Jackknife analysis:** Removing any single DIRECT match from the 43-event subset shifts p to 0.18. Removing any pair shifts p above 0.05 in all 15 combinations. This fragility is a consequence of small n, not of an unstable ratio: with only 6 hits, each hit carries substantial weight.

An additional 6 events fall within 30 days of a retrograde boundary (PROXIMATE), bringing the total to 12/43 (27.9%) within the retrograde-plus-shadow window, compared to an expected 17.5% (p = 0.061).

**Implication:** The observed ratio (~1.9x) is robust to the removal of date-uncertain events. The path to a definitive result is not refining dates but expanding the database: at n = 150 with the same 1.9x ratio, the binomial test would yield p < 0.005.

## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 The Sign-Independent Signal

The most important finding of this study is that the displacement correlation operates at the level of Venus retrograde motion itself, not at the level of any specific zodiacal sign or 8-year series. The 11 DIRECT matches span eight signs, and the binomial test reaches significance (p = 0.033) only when all retrogrades are pooled.

This has implications for astrological theory. If the correlation is real, it suggests that the condition of retrograde motion, classically understood as an inversion or disruption of the planet's ordinary significations, carries thematic content independent of the zodiacal context. Venus retrograde in Taurus (its own domicile) correlates with displacement just as Venus retrograde in Scorpio (its detriment) does. The disruption is intrinsic to the retrograde condition, not to the sign.

### 4.2 The Series Question as a Secondary Layer

The series analysis reveals additional structure within the sign-independent signal. Two series (B and E) show above-expected nearest-retrograde counts, but neither reaches individual significance. This is consistent with the primary finding: the signal is distributed across all five series and is strong enough to detect only when pooled.

The distinct patterns of Series B (crisis-concentrated) and Series E (historically distributed) may reflect different mechanisms or may be artifacts of event selection. The database expansion from 64 to 82 events revealed both patterns, with each expansion enriching rather than dissolving the observed correlations.

### 4.3 The Precessional Dimension

The period-stratified analysis suggests a possible secondary effect: whichever series occupies Scorpio at a given era tends to show above-expected displacement correlation. However, this observation rests on small per-period samples and cannot be formally tested with the current database. It remains a hypothesis for future investigation.

### 4.4 Theoretical Framework

The interpretive framework derives from classical sources:

- **Venus** signifies social consensus (Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos* I.4; Abu Ma'shar, *Great Introduction* IV)
- **Retrograde motion** signifies disruption of ordinary significations (Bonatti, *Liber Astronomiae* VI; Lilly, *Christian Astrology* I)

Forced displacement can be understood as social consensus enforcement through exclusion: the community defines itself by whom it expels. The sign-independent nature of the correlation supports the classical emphasis on retrograde as a condition in its own right, distinct from dignities or debilities related to zodiacal position.

### 4.5 Comparison with Previous Work

The author's earthquake study (Uysal, 2026) analyzed 3,475 events and produced a clear null result. The present study, with n = 82, produces a statistically significant result for the primary test (p = 0.033). The two studies differ in important ways: earthquakes are geophysical events with no obvious social dimension, while forced displacement is a social phenomenon whose connection to Venus (social consensus) has classical theoretical support. The contrast between the null earthquake result and the significant displacement result is itself noteworthy.

### 4.6 Limitations

1. **Post hoc hypothesis:** The initial observation was generated from the data.
2. **Event selection:** The database reflects the author's knowledge and geographic focus.
3. **Date precision:** Ancient events are dated to estimated months, not exact days.
4. **Multiple testing:** The primary binomial test was not pre-registered.
5. **Sample size:** n = 82 is sufficient for the primary test but marginal for per-series analysis.

The p = 0.033 result, while below the conventional 0.05 threshold, should be interpreted with caution given the exploratory design. Pre-registered replication with an independently compiled database is essential.

## 5. Conclusion

**Primary finding:** Venus retrograde periods correlate with forced displacement events at 1.87 times the expected rate (11/82 = 13.4% vs. expected 7.2%). Binomial test p = 0.033, statistically significant. The correlation spans eight zodiacal signs and operates independently of any specific sign or series.

**Secondary finding:** Within the five 8-year synodic series, Series B (Capricorn, 22/82) and Series E (Scorpio-Libra, 17/82) show above-expected counts with distinct clustering patterns. Neither reaches individual significance; the significant signal emerges only when all retrogrades are pooled.

**Precessional pattern:** The dominant series shifts across historical periods in a pattern partially consistent with Venus retrograde in Scorpio, its detriment sign.

**Strongest individual correlation:** Kristallnacht (November 9-10, 1938), day 10 of a 41-day Series E retrograde.

**Methodological contribution:** This study demonstrates the application of binomial testing, Swiss Ephemeris computation, and systematic control group comparison to mundane astrological research.

**Future directions:** Pre-registered replication with independently compiled database (n > 150); formal test of the Scorpio hypothesis; cross-cycle comparison with Mars retrograde and Saturn-Neptune; investigation of whether the retrograde correlation extends to other categories of social disruption.
